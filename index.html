Red Foxes





Where to Find Me

The red fox is one of the most widely distributed species of Carnivora. They can be found across the entire Northern Hemisphere. In North America, red foxes are native and can be found throughout the United States and most of Canada (except far up north- this is home to the artic fox).



Hanging Out at the Den

Red foxes live in many diverse habitats. They can exist in forests, grasslands, mountains, deserts, and even human environments such as farms and suburban areas. They make their homes in the ground. They dig burrows, which are called dens, and this is their safe spot to sleep, store food, and have pups.



The Fox's Look

When most people think of a fox, they probably picture the red fox. The red fox has dense fur, most of which is a rusty red color. They also can have white and black in their coloration. Male foxes weigh 10-12 pounds on average, while females weigh 9-10 pounds. When the kits (i.e. baby foxes) are born, they have grey fur for most of the first month of their lives. Their new adult-colored coat comes in shortly after.



I'm Not Just Cute...

Red foxes have quite the personality. They are playful, smart, curious, and adaptable. They are said to have a personality somewhere between the domestic dog and the domestic cat. They are known to play with objects such as balls or garden pinwheels. Kits can oftentimes be find just outside the den playing together.



A Fox's Life

Red foxes mate in the winter and give birth in the spring. The litter size can be from two kits all the way up to 12 kits. Both the male and female foxes care for the kits until they leave the den in autumn. The average lifespan of the red fox in the wild is 2-4 years.
